Tunnbröd

Rye Flatbread

Description

The Vikings crafted a diet rooted in simplicity and nutritional value.
To complement their protein-rich diet, Vikings turned to grains.
Grains provided a reliable source of energy for their active lifestyles.
They could also be kept for months, and were easily transported.
Grains were used to make bread, porridge, and beer.
Barley, a hardy versatile grain, was a common presence,
forming the basis of porridges and bread. Other grains were used,
such as rye as featured in this recipe.

Flatbreads were among the different breads baked with grains.
These small breads were made of flour, eggs and water.
They were baked in a pan or grilled over the fire. It was not until
the end of the Viking period that ovens were widely used in the household.
These breads are good with all Viking foods and just as delicious on their own.
You can top them with whatever you’d like
– cheese, cured meats, or even honey, butter, or Skyr.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of rye flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • salt (a pinch)
  • butter for cooking (optional)

Steps

  1. Mix the rye flour and salt in a large bowl.
    Gradually add water, mixing until you form a firm, not sticky, dough.
    You may need slightly less or more water, depending on your flour.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls about the size of a golf ball.
    On a well-floured surface, roll out each ball as thin as possible.
  3. Heat a dry skillet over medium-high heat.
    You can lightly butter the skillet, but traditionally,
    this bread was baked without fat.
  4. Cook each flatbread for about 2-3 minutes on each side or
    until they are golden and have small charred spots.
    They should puff up a bit while cooking. Serve warm.
