Gröt and Cod

Barley Porridge and Cod

Description

Fish was among the most common foods eaten during Viking times. This largely consisted of cod, herring, and salmon caught along the long Scandinavian coastline. Dried fish was likely a frequent ingredient in Viking-age cooking because it could be stored for long periods without spoiling. Grains could also be kept for months, and easily transported, which means they were also a likely staple of a traveling Viking's diet. They were used to make bread, porridge, and beer.

This dish combines stock from dried fish (cod), with shallots, barley, dill seeds and malt vinegar. As a finished meal, this version of porridge looks more like a risotto than the oat porridge that adorns modern breakfast tables. Upon taste, it ticks all the boxes important for Viking age meals: it's surprisingly packed with flavour, filling and warm, perfect on a frigid day.
