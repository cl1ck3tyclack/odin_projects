Traveler's Gröt

Cod Stock Barley Porridge

Description

Fish was among the most common foods eaten during Viking times.
This largely consisted of cod, herring, and salmon caught along the long Scandinavian coastline.
Dried fish was likely a frequent ingredient in Viking-age cooking because it could be stored for
long periods without spoiling. Grains could also be kept for months, and easily transported,
which means they were also a likely staple of a traveling Viking's diet.
They were used to make bread, porridge, and beer.

This dish combines stock from dried fish (cod), with shallots, barley, dill seeds and malt vinegar.
As a finished meal, this version of porridge looks more like a risotto than the oat porridge that adorns
modern breakfast tables. Upon taste, it ticks all the boxes important for Viking age meals:
it's surprisingly packed with flavour, filling and warm, perfect on a frigid day.

Ingredients

For the stock:

  • 1 dried cod head
  • 2-3 litres of water

For the porridge:

  • 1¾ cups of barley
  • 5 tbsp salted butter
  • 4-5 shallots, finely chopped
  • ½ - 1 tsp dill seeds
  • 1 tbsp malt vinegar

Steps

  1. To make the stock, crush the cod head on a hard surface.
    Breaking it into pieces ensures you will maximize the flavor it gives the stock.
    To make the stock, put the crushed cod head into a pot with the water and bring it to the boil.
    Let it simmer at a low temperature for 90 minutes, or until the water is reduced to about half.
    Drain and discard the solids, then keep the stock warm.
  2. In the meantime, crush or grind the barley.
  3. Melt the butter in a medium or large pot, then add the shallots and sweat them over medium heat
    until they become translucent (not browned). Add the dill seeds to the butter and shallot mixture,
    stirring once or twice and reduce to low to medium heat. Add the barley and mix thoroughly.
    Pour in the malt vinegar and stir. The grains should absorb the vinegar. Add more if you prefer
    your dish on the more acidic side.
  4. Once the stock is ready, pour in enough to cover the grains. Stir and add more, little by little.
    Let the pot simmer on low to medium heat until the grains look soft and creamy. Serve warm.
